'Not so independent when it matters are we jocksville?' Outrage as Katie Hopkins jokes about Scotland sending Ebola patient back to England for treatment 

  • Nurse was flown from Glasgow to London to be treated for Ebola today
  • Katie Hopkins used case as excuse to joke about Scottish independence
  • Television personality said 'sweaty jocks' were sending 'Ebola bombs' 
  • People have branded her 'vile' and Piers Morgan said she was 'repulsive' 
  • Nurse has been taken to Royal Free Hospital in London which has special unit for treating the deadly disease and to prevent further infection 
  • She had been working in Sierra Leone and fell ill when she arrived home  

By Claire Carter for MailOnline

Published: 05:17 EST, 30 December 2014 | Updated: 07:19 EST, 30 December 2014

Television personality Katie Hopkins has been branded 'vile' and a racist after she posted a joke about Scotland sending a patient with Ebola to an English hospital for treatment. 

A female nurse who had been treating patients for the deadly disease in Sierra Leone, was transferred from her home town of Glasgow to London for treatment after she tested positive for the disease.

But after news of her condition spread, the former Apprentice contestant took to Twitter to make jibes about Scottish independence because the woman is being treated in London.

Scroll down for video 

Television personality Katie Hopkins provoked outrage after she made jibes about Scottish independence after she learnt that a patient with Ebola had been moved from Glasgow to London for treatment

Hopkins wrote: 'Glaswegian ebola patient moved to London's Royal Free Hospital. Not so independent when it matters most are we jocksville?'

She added: 'Little sweaty jocks, sending us Ebola bombs in the form of sweaty Glaswegians just isn't cricket.' 

Her remarks caused outrage on Twitter, with one user asking 'just how long can you stoop?' Others accused her of political point scoring.

This is not the first time Hopkins has made offensive remarks about Ebola. In October she said the disease was an efficient way to control the population. 

The female nurse fell ill on only hours after returning home from Africa having flown back to Scotland from Africa via Heathrow.

She was transferred from Glasgow to Heathrow early on Monday morning to be treated at the Royal Free Hospital in London, which has a special containment unit for Ebola patients.

But just hours after news of the patient arriving in London was revealed, Hopkins seized on the opportunity to make comments about Scottish independence.
